# Mission

> ## Free yourself from repetitive work.

Anyone should be able to hand off the repetitive tasks they no longer want to
do — and have them done, on their own, around the clock.

Myra's bet is that the AI automation power belongs to **everyone**: you describe
what you want done, you delegate it, and it runs without you.

## How we say it

The mission is the *why* and never changes. The lines below are how it shows up
today, as a developer-first product, and will evolve as the audience widens.

| Layer | Line | Scope |
|-------|------|-------|
| **Mission** | Free yourself from repetitive work. | The why — the world we're after. Outlives the product. |
| **Positioning** | Your AI workforce, running 24/7. | The what — how Myra presents today. |
| **Tagline** | Delegate the repetitive work. · Agents that work while you sleep. | The hook. |

These nest, they don't compete: the *workforce* is **how**; freeing yourself from
repetitive work is **why**.

## How we know we're winning (KPI)

The win isn't usage — it's work that stops landing on a human. The day someone
sets up an agent, walks away, and never thinks about that task again, we've done
our job. So we hold ourselves to a handful of numbers, and only these:

- **Hours of repetitive work deleted** — the headline; everything else serves it.
  Counted from agent runs fired on a schedule, each weighted by the human time it
- **Tasks running autonomously** — set once, no one babysitting, no changes to do.
- **Agents set up once and never touched again** — they worked well enough that
  they never needed a fix. Measured as agents that keep producing clean runs with
  zero edits to their prompt, preset, or schedule after the day they were created.
